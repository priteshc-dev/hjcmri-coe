Introduction & Background
For more than ten years, RIHAN (Rural Initiative for Health and Nutrition) has worked closely with rural families around Ranjangaon to bridge daily gaps in health and nutrition. What started as a few health camps has expanded into an integrated programme that now serves over 30,000 people across more than 24 villages and wadi/wasti settlements.

Our emphasis is on practicality and a people-centered approach: conducting early screenings, providing counsel, ensuring consistent follow-up, and connecting every household to exemplary services, whether public or private. Currently, RIHAN supports children, pregnant women, new mothers, and elders through a network of village representatives, government partners, and trained volunteers.

Under this umbrella, YUVAAN is our adolescent health and nutrition initiative, which equips 10‚Äì19-year-olds with nutrition knowledge, life skills, and confidence to make informed choices.
